I Want to Share My Story

November 7, 2024 • Personal • 5 min read
Back to Home

I want to share more about myself. Recently, more people have been following me on social media, and I'm truly grateful. Some are curious about what kind of person I am... That's why I started this blog - to help people know me better.

This will take just a few minutes to read, so please bear with me for a moment 💭

My Swimming Dreams

From kindergarten, I trained intensely for swimming. My dream was to become an Olympic athlete, and I dedicated my entire childhood to this goal. Every day after school, I would head straight to the pool, practicing stroke after stroke, pushing my limits.

Swimming wasn't just a sport for me - it was my identity, my purpose, my everything. I lived and breathed for those moments in the water, feeling the rhythm of my strokes and the satisfaction of improving my times.

When Everything Changed

At age 13-14, my body began to change in ways I wasn't prepared for. As a competitive swimmer, these changes affected not just how I looked, but how I performed in the water. What had once felt natural and effortless suddenly became a source of self-consciousness and struggle.

"The pool that had once been my sanctuary suddenly felt like a place where I was exposed and vulnerable."

I started experiencing comments and looks that made me uncomfortable. The sport I had loved so passionately became a source of anxiety. I found myself trying to hide, feeling ashamed of the very body that had carried me through years of competition.

The Decision to Walk Away

Eventually, the emotional weight became too much to bear. I made the incredibly difficult decision to quit competitive swimming - walking away from the Olympic dreams that had defined my entire childhood. It felt like losing a part of myself.

For a while, I tried to become invisible. I avoided situations where I might be noticed or judged. The confident athlete I had once been seemed like a distant memory.

Finding My Way Back

It wasn't until college that things began to change. A talent agency approached me, and for the first time in years, someone saw something positive in me that I couldn't see myself. They encouraged me to embrace who I am, not hide from it.

Social media became an unexpected turning point. The supportive comments and encouragement from people who followed my journey helped me slowly rebuild my confidence. I began to realize that the very things I had been ashamed of could actually be sources of strength.

My Mission Now

Today, I'm committed to challenging myself in new ways and inspiring others who might be struggling with similar insecurities. I want to show that it's possible to overcome self-doubt and learn to love yourself for who you truly are.

Every day is still a journey of growth for me. There are moments when those old insecurities creep back in, but I've learned that growth isn't about being perfect - it's about being willing to keep moving forward, even when it's scary.

"Our perceived weaknesses can become our greatest sources of strength when we learn to embrace them."

If you're reading this and struggling with your own insecurities or self-doubt, I want you to know that you're not alone. Your journey is valid, your feelings are real, and you have the strength within you to overcome whatever challenges you're facing.

Thank you for taking the time to read my story. This blog is my space to continue sharing this journey of growth, and I hope it can be a source of encouragement for others walking similar paths.

With love and gratitude,
Risa ✨
