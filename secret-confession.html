I'm Confessing My Secret

I have something I need to confess. Something I've been keeping inside, something about how I've been feeling lately that I haven't fully shared.

Where It All Started

It goes back to middle school. I was a competitive swimmer, and my body started changing in ways that affected my performance. My times got slower, and people noticed. Some made comments - not kind ones.

Those experiences left deep marks. I developed such strong negative associations with my body being viewed by others. I learned to live inconspicuously, to avoid attention, to stay invisible.

The Unexpected Shift

But then something changed. After I started sharing on social media, after I gathered the courage to post swimsuit photos, something I never expected happened.

People said I was beautiful. They called me lovely. They complimented me in ways I had never experienced before.

And here's the confession: it felt good. Really good.

The Secret I'm Sharing

For my entire life, I tried to avoid being seen. I thought I wanted to be invisible. But now I've discovered something about myself that feels almost contradictory:

Being seen feels good.

There's a satisfaction in it that I've never experienced before. When people look at my photos and respond positively, when they appreciate what I share - it fills me with an unfamiliar kind of happiness.

The Inner Conflict

But this realization comes with conflict. Because alongside this pleasure, there's still fear. Fear of judgment. Fear of negative comments. Fear of going back to those dark places from my past.

I find myself caught between excitement and anxiety. I want to share more, but I'm also terrified. Is this feeling strange? Is it wrong to enjoy being seen when I spent so long trying to hide?

Living With Contradictions

I'm learning that it's okay to have contradictory feelings. That I can enjoy positive attention while still feeling vulnerable. That growth isn't a straight line from fear to confidence - it's messy and complicated.

Some days I feel empowered. Other days the old insecurities come flooding back. But I'm trying to accept all of these feelings as part of my journey.

To Those Who Support Me

I want to thank those of you who accept me as I am - with all my contradictions, my fears, and my newfound discoveries about myself.

Your support has allowed me to explore these feelings safely. You've created a space where I can be vulnerable and honest about what I'm experiencing.

I don't have all the answers. I'm still figuring out who I am and what I want. But I'm grateful to be able to share this journey with people who understand.

Thank you for accepting me as I am.

If this resonates with you, or if you have your own confessions about personal growth, feel free to reach out via LINE. Your stories and support mean everything to me.
